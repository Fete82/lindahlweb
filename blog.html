Who I Am and Why I Study

2021-09-28

My name is Edvin Lindahl and I like CSS.

Yesterday at 3:50 pm., my girlfriend came home from her job. "Why aren't you at work?" she asked, surprised.

Panic. My shift was due to start at 4 pm. I had completely forgotten. In all my years of working random gigs in production or logistics, that has happened 0 times. It happened yesterday because I was busy making this website. After the initial panic, as well as some kicking myself for lack of virtue, I was suddenly filled with joy. The fact that I had forgotten was a strong indicator that the path of CMS is the path I'm supposed to be on.

True Story. I was only 10 minutes late by the way. I don't think anyone at work even noticed.

Before coming to study the art of development in the virtual multiverse, I studied Practical Philosophy and English at Lund University. I wanted to be a teacher, but I abandoned that when I realised I'm more deeply drawn to creation than teaching.

Like everyone else in this industry, I hope to be able to develop the necessary skills required to eventually be able to launch and run a succesful business. If my natural composition and talent prove to be ill-aligned with such a fate, I will surely do well enough as a regular code monkey.

"Inflation Is Transitory."

2021-09-28

In 1971, president Nixon took the United States off the gold standard, effectively severing the tie between the dollar and gold. American dollars could no longer be traded in for gold, and from this time until today the USD is backed by nothing but a rapidly increasing government debt.

At the turn of the twentieth century, there was some $7 billion in existence. In 1971, the US money supply stood at $480 billion. Today, in 2021, it's over $20 trillion. That is more than 40 times higher than in 1971, and over 2,800 times higher than in 1900.
